NEAT (Next-Generation Effective Affective Technology) Research Lab

This research lab is headed by Dr. Shameem Ahmed and Dr. Moushumi Sharmin

The research lab focuses on designing technology to improve the life experience of autistic individuals. This technology addresses economic and socially-critical problems, such as creating accessible employment opportunities for autistic college students and real-time detection of stress and anxiety in autistic young adults to improve their mental health conditions.

eXpress

A significant proportion of autistic individuals are considered 'non-verbal or minimally verbal' and have difficulty expressing their emotions. Commercial alternative and assistive communication (AAC) applications are not only scarce, but also often ineffective in aiding autistic individuals in expressing emotions due to a minimal understanding of their unique needs and preferences.

Therefore, as part of my undergraduate research, I helped develop eXpress, a mobile application that helps autistic individuals communicate their emotions through customizable symbolic representations of emotions via emoticons, musical notes, auditory feedback, and color.

This resulted in a poster publication at UbiComp 2022: eXpress Poster

Smartwatch Data Collection

One of the research projects featured biological data collection (including GPS and Heart rate variability) using smartwatches. For some time, data collection was interrupted, and I was assigned to the project to discover the reason for the interruption.

The cause turned out to be the serverless solution that the smartwatch sent data to through an HTTP request, which then formatted the data and sent it to the database. The free tier that was hosting our apps was sunset (discontinued) and all the apps were deleted.

My contribution was using my then-recently-acquired Azure Fundamentals certification to recreate the entire deleted codebase using Azure Function Apps, updating the HTTP endpoints, and then testing to ensure that the format in the database was still consistent.

A few proper publications are on the horizon.

Panic Pal

Panic Pal is a React Native application with ChatGPT integration I'm working on for my CSCI 536: Technology for Social Good class. Panic attacks are sudden psychological episodes that have frightening symptoms such as shortness of breath, palpitations, or a feeling of doom or loss of control. These intense episodes of fear and anxiety can lead to social isolation, impaired relationships, and reduced quality of life.

Breathing/grounding techniques and interventions help to alleviate these issues. These techniques can be hard to carry out by yourself. Our app aims to provide ease in managing panic attacks in the moment to empower individuals to better cope with and reduce the impact panic attacks can have on their life.

My contributions, among others, include:

  • Firebase Database integration
  • User accounts and login screen using Firebase Authentication
  • Settings page with settings stored on local storage
  • Azure Function App used to call ChatGPT API to not expose the API key
  • Breathing intervention page implementation
